About

I’m a PhD student in mathematics at the Technical University of Dresden, Germany. In my copious free time I wish. I like to hack on free software—especially anything involving Haskell or Emacs—play the guitar, ride my bike around, or watch classic movies. I also spend way too much money on (good) coffee and tea.

My work in mathematics can broadly be characterised under the term “category theory and its applications”. In particular, I like thinking about things like Hopf monads, graphical calculi, duoidal and linearly distributive categories, as well as operads. If you’re interested in my publications, you can check out my name on the arXiv. I’ve also given some talks, slides of which are available here.
